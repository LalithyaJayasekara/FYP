## 1.0 Executive Summary
This proposal recommends implementation of an AI-enabled Healthcare-HR Bridge that unifies clinical assessment, intervention, and workplace accommodation planning for individuals with learning disorders. The current process is fragmented across healthcare and HR, resulting in delayed support, duplicated work, and weaker outcomes.

The preferred option is an integrated, consent-governed platform with a shared individual profile, coordinated recommendations, and bidirectional feedback loops. The project is proposed for phased delivery over 12 months, with a post-pilot scale phase. The option is viable based on strategic fit, expected benefits, and manageable risk.

## 2.0 Introduction and Summary
### 2.1 Subject
Development and deployment of a Healthcare-HR Bridge platform linking clinical and occupational decision-making for individuals with learning disorders.

### 2.2 Rationale for Preferred Option
The preferred option is selected because it:
- Resolves the root cause of fragmented support pathways.
- Establishes a single source of truth for coordinated decisions.
- Aligns clinical treatment with workplace accommodations.
- Produces measurable cross-domain outcomes.
- Supports scalable deployment through API-first design.

### 2.3 Current Business Process (Problematic Landscape)
Current-state characteristics:
- Healthcare diagnosis and treatment decisions are made independently.
- HR receives static information late in the process.
- Accommodation decisions are often generic and clinically disconnected.
- Clinical teams receive limited occupational performance feedback.

Current-state impacts:
- Slow activation of practical support.
- Inconsistent intervention quality.
- Lower adherence and reduced overall effectiveness.
- Higher administrative overhead and avoidable productivity losses.

### 2.4 Description of the Problem
There is no integrated decision pathway connecting clinical findings to workplace action in real time. This causes serial delays, contradictory recommendations, poor intervention visibility, and repeated reassessment.

### 2.5 Opportunity
The project creates a coordinated model where diagnosis and workplace planning are triggered together, evidence is shared securely, and outcomes are measured using linked clinical and occupational metrics.

### 2.6 Project Objectives
- Build a unified profile with consent-controlled clinical and occupational data.
- Provide AI-assisted and clinician-validated recommendations.
- Coordinate intervention and accommodation plans through synchronized workflows.
- Track adherence and outcomes across both domains.
- Improve time-to-support, support quality, retention, and performance.

### 2.7 Project Scope
In scope:
- Unified profile, consent engine, role-based access.
- Clinical and occupational module integration.
- Coordinated recommendation engine.
- Intervention tracking and outcomes dashboards.
- Standards-based API integration with EHR/HRIS systems.

Out of scope (initial release):
- Full multi-country legal customization.
- Broad industry-specific template library.
- Full research publication workflow automation.

### 2.8 Business Benefits
Primary benefits:
- Faster transition from assessment to workplace support.
- Higher-quality and personalized accommodations.
- Better convergence of clinical progress and work performance.
- Lower productivity losses from unmanaged conditions.
- Improved employee engagement and retention.

Strategic benefits:
- Improved compliance posture via consent and audit trails.
- Stronger evidence-based HR and clinical decision practices.
- Creation of a long-term data asset for predictive improvement.

## 4.0 Approach
The project uses a socio-technical approach that combines process redesign, technology delivery, and governance assurance.

Approach principles:
- Human-in-the-loop: AI supports decisions; clinicians validate clinical conclusions.
- Privacy-by-design: consent boundaries and minimal disclosure by default.
- Interoperability-first: standards-based APIs for existing systems.
- Outcome-driven delivery: prioritize measurable impact.

### 4.1 Assessment of Impact on Privacy
Data categories:
- Sensitive health data.
- Occupational performance indicators.
- Consent and access metadata.
- Audit and security logs.

Key privacy risks:
- Unauthorized cross-domain access.
- Over-sharing of sensitive clinical details.
- Excessive retention of personally identifiable data.
- Secondary data use without explicit consent.

Mitigation controls:
- Granular and time-bound consent controls.
- Role-based and purpose-based authorization.
- Encryption in transit and at rest.
- Immutable audit logging and monitoring.
- Automatic consent expiry and revocation enforcement.
- Data minimization and retention schedules.

Residual privacy impact rating:
- Medium after controls, acceptable with recurring compliance oversight.

### 4.2 Assumptions and Constraints
Assumptions:
- Stakeholders provide timely requirements clarification.
- Pilot organizations provide representative data and users.
- Integration endpoints for EHR/HRIS are available.

Constraints:
- Limited project timeline within academic schedule.
- Restricted access to production-grade sensitive datasets.
- Budget assumptions are indicative until formally approved.

## 5.0 Preferred Alternative
Preferred alternative: Integrated Healthcare-HR Bridge (real-time synchronized model).

Alternative options considered:
- Option A: Maintain existing siloed process with minor workflow changes.
- Option B: One-way data handoff from healthcare to HR only.
- Option C: Full bidirectional integrated bridge (preferred).

Selection rationale:
- Option C offers the strongest problem-solution fit and highest projected outcome improvement.

### 5.1 Business Benefits
Target benefits aligned to project requirements:
- Time to diagnosis/support pathway: 8 weeks to 3 weeks.
- Accommodation implementation time: 4 weeks to 1 week.
- Treatment compliance: 60% to 85%.
- Job placement or role-fit success: 65% to 85%.
- Retention for supported employees: 70% to 88%.
- Coordinated decision alignment: >= 88%.

### 5.2 Severity Calculation and Metrics
Severity model for case prioritization:
- Severity Score = (Clinical Severity x 0.40) + (Occupational Impact x 0.35) + (Urgency x 0.15) + (Adherence Risk x 0.10)
- Component scale: 1-5 each.
- Total scale: 1.0-5.0.

Severity bands:
- 4.0-5.0: Critical (immediate intervention).
- 3.0-3.9: High (intervention within 5 business days).
- 2.0-2.9: Moderate (planned intervention cycle).
- 1.0-1.9: Low (monitor and optimize).

Performance measurement framework:
- Clinical: symptom reduction, adherence, reassessment improvement.
- Occupational: task completion, error trend, manager evaluations.
- Bridge: sync latency, recommendation acceptance, alignment rate, linked-outcome correlation.
- Privacy: blocked unauthorized access, consent breaches, audit completeness.

## 6.0 Risk Management Plan
Risk management applies throughout design, build, pilot, and scale phases. Risks are reviewed monthly with formal escalation thresholds.

### 6.1 Risk Assessment
| Risk | Likelihood | Impact | Rating |
|---|---|---|---|
| Data privacy breach or misconfigured access | Medium | High | High |
| Integration delays with EHR/HRIS systems | High | Medium | High |
| Low user adoption by clinicians or HR | Medium | High | High |
| Recommendation bias or model drift | Medium | High | High |
| Regulatory interpretation changes | Low | Medium | Medium |
| Budget overrun from scope increase | Medium | Medium | Medium |
| Poor data quality impacting recommendations | Medium | Medium | Medium |

### 6.2 Risk Response
- Privacy risk: enforce least privilege, penetration testing, incident response procedures.
- Integration risk: staged connectors, technical fallback workflows, milestone gating.
- Adoption risk: pilot champions, role-based training, feedback loops.
- Model risk: fairness tests, drift monitoring, retraining plan, explainability checks.
- Compliance risk: policy review checkpoints and legal consultation.
- Budget risk: change control process, contingency governance.
- Data quality risk: validation rules and anomaly alerts.

## 7.0 Conclusion and Recommendations
The business case supports proceeding with the integrated Healthcare-HR Bridge as the preferred investment. It addresses a clear operational gap, provides measurable benefit potential, and introduces a scalable model for coordinated support.

Recommendations:
- Approve phased implementation with governance oversight.
- Run a controlled pilot with predefined acceptance criteria.
- Track bridge-specific KPIs as primary success indicators.
- Enforce privacy and compliance controls as release gates.
- Approve scale-up only after pilot KPI validation.

## 8.0 Proposal Acceptance Criteria
The proposal is considered successful when all criteria below are met:
- Minimum viable integrated workflow delivered by Month 8.
- Pilot completion by Month 12 with documented outcomes.
- KPI movement meets or exceeds 70% of target improvements in pilot cohort.
- No critical privacy incidents during pilot period.
- Supervisor review and sign-off completed.
